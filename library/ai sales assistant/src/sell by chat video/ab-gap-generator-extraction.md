## ‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Warning: Question Selection

### **The Fatal Mistake**
**Original Teaching**: "Now I will say the mistake a lot of people make is they ask a question that the prospect or the buyer can't answer. Okay? This happens in software world all the time. They're selling software and they're like, how much do you, how much storage do you use right now for your video content? And then the buyer can't answer that question, you just stop the sale. Okay? You never ask a question that you don't think the buyer can actually answer."

**Implementation Rule**: Only ask gap questions the prospect can confidently answer without research.

### **Conversation-Killing Examples**
**Storage Question**: "How much storage do you use right now for your video content?"
**Problem**: Requires technical knowledge prospect doesn't have

**Net Promoter Score**: "What's your net promoter score? What do you want it to be? Well, we have never sent one."
**Problem**: Requires data prospect hasn't collected

### **The "Get Ready" Problem**
**Original Teaching**: "So they won't work or buy from you until they go do the thing that you just identified is the thing they didn't even do. So they gotta go get ready to work with you. How many of you guys have had people say to you, I gotta get ready to work with you? Or some version of that."

**Warning**: Gap questions that require preparation create delay and often prevent sales.

## üö´ Question Safety Protocol

### **Green Light Questions** (Safe to Ask)
- Questions about easily observable metrics
- Questions about personal experience/feelings
- Questions about clear business numbers they track
- Questions about obvious current states

### **Red Light Questions** (Conversation Killers)
- Technical specifications they haven't measured
- Data they would need to research
- Industry benchmarks they don't know
- Complex calculations they can't do

### **Safety Check Process**
Before suggesting gap questions, AI evaluates:
1. "Can the prospect answer this immediately?"
2. "Does this require research or data collection?"
3. "Would this create a 'get ready to work with you' response?"
