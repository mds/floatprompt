## ‚ö° The First Critical Failure: Don't Qualify Upfront

*From "The Five F-You's" - The foundation of wasted time and poor results*

### Qualification Failure Problem

**The Issue:**
"They don't qualify upfront. They don't qualify. They do a bunch of calls with people that they shouldn't be talking to. You get a prospect, you get a lead, you're like, I'll do a call. I'll do a call. You got hour call meetings with people that should never been on the call."

**Professional Standard:**
Never schedule sales conversations without proper qualification parameters.

### Upfront Qualification Requirements

**Before Scheduling Criteria:**
- Decision-making authority confirmed
- Budget parameters established
- Timeline and urgency assessed
- Genuine need and interest validated

**Qualification Questions for Scheduling:**
- "Are you the person who makes decisions about [relevant area]?"
- "What's your timeline for making a decision on this?"
- "Do you have a budget allocated for this type of solution?"
- "What's driving the urgency to solve this now?"

**Disqualification Standards:**
- No decision-making authority
- No realistic budget or investment capacity
- No genuine urgency or timeline pressure
- Unclear or manufactured need

## üîç The Three Essential Discovery Questions

*"Those are my three favorite questions. Who do you serve? What do you do and how does it work?"*

### Question #1: "Who do you serve?"

**Primary Question:**
"Who do you serve? Who's a perfect customer for you?"

**Purpose:**
- Understand their target market and customer base
- Assess business model and market positioning
- Identify potential alignment with your solutions
- Gauge business sophistication and focus

**Follow-up Probing:**
- "What characteristics make someone your ideal client?"
- "How do you currently find these customers?"
- "What size companies/individuals do you typically work with?"
- "What industries or sectors do you focus on?"

### Question #2: "What do you do for them?"

**Primary Question:**
"What do you do for them?"

**Purpose:**
- Understand their value proposition and service offering
- Assess business model and revenue streams
- Identify potential solution integration points
- Evaluate business complexity and sophistication

**Follow-up Probing:**
- "What specific results do you deliver?"
- "What's your primary service or product offering?"
- "How do you measure success for your clients?"
- "What makes your approach different or unique?"

### Question #3: "How does that work?"

**Primary Question:**
"How does that work?"

**Purpose:**
- Understand their operational processes and systems
- Assess business infrastructure and capabilities
- Identify operational challenges and improvement opportunities
- Evaluate scalability and growth potential

**Follow-up Probing:**
- "Walk me through your typical process"
- "What systems and tools do you use?"
- "How do you deliver that to clients?"
- "What's your team structure for this?"

## ü©∫ Deep Digging Methodology

*"They don't dig deep enough. When you're going through their current challenges, their roadblocks, their reality, like double click, ask them the impact of the current situation."*

### The Fourth Critical Failure: Don't Dig Deep Enough

**Problem:**
"They don't dig deep enough. When you're going through their current challenges, their roadblocks, their reality, like double click."

**Professional Requirement:**
"Ask them the impact of the current situation. You know, what is your biggest fear when you're building a new home? What's your biggest concern really? Has that ever happened to a friend of yours? Oh wow. What happen? Let them sit in that fear."

### Deep Digging Question Framework

**Impact Assessment Questions:**
- "What's the impact of this current situation?"
- "How is this affecting your business/life/goals?"
- "What happens if this doesn't get resolved?"
- "What's your biggest fear about this situation?"

**Fear and Concern Exploration:**
- "What's your biggest concern about [their situation]?"
- "What keeps you up at night about this?"
- "What are you most worried about?"
- "What's the worst-case scenario if nothing changes?"

**Social Proof and Validation:**
- "Has this ever happened to someone you know?"
- "Have you seen others deal with this problem?"
- "What happened to them?"
- "How did that turn out?"

**Pain Amplification Technique:**
"Let them sit in that fear. Let them, you have to. If you don't, they won't make a decision."

### Professional Pain Exploration Standards

**Sitting in the Pain:**
- Don't rush to solutions or comfort
- Allow emotional impact to be fully experienced
- Let them express frustration and concern completely
- Build sufficient discomfort with status quo

**Double-Click Methodology:**
- Ask follow-up questions to every pain point
- Explore ripple effects and secondary impacts
- Investigate timeline and urgency factors
- Assess emotional and business consequences

**Professional Boundary:**
"You can't help somebody if they don't get into your world. Think of all the people that did, decided not to invest in themselves to be on this call. I can't teach them this. They need to lean in. If they don't pay, they don't pay attention."
