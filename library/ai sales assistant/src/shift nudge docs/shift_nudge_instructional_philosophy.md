## üéì Teaching as Orientation ‚Üí Extraction ‚Üí Structure

### üß† Orientation (Map)
- We begin every lesson with territory. We give students a reason to care.
- Orientation is not a "welcome slide" ‚Äî it is cognitive grounding.
- Where does this problem show up? Why does it matter? Why should you give your attention to this?

### üß† Extraction (Decide)
- Once students are oriented, we isolate what matters.
- We do not teach everything. We teach the pieces that count.
- The "what" is always scoped intentionally. There is no noise.
- This phase often includes examples, references, and critical distinctions.

### üß† Structure (Build)
- Then we build. This is not just the result ‚Äî it‚Äôs the reusable system.
- The lesson's final third shows how the knowledge gets constructed, reused, or applied.
- If Map and Decide were respected, the student can now build without chaos.
